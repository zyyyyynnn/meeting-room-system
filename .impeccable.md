## Design Context

### Users
This system is used by internal employees, administrators, and super administrators throughout the workday to book meeting rooms, approve requests, review notifications, and keep day-to-day operations moving with minimal friction. The interface should help them scan quickly, trust what they see, and complete tasks without visual noise.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel quiet, refined, and orderly. It should evoke confidence, control, and calm focus rather than startup flashiness or consumer-playful energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-led luxury editorial direction built on parchment-like neutrals, warm stone surfaces, deep olive accents, and restrained copper warmth. Headlines should carry more presence through serif typography, while body copy stays highly legible with a clean sans-serif. Motion should feel smooth and deliberate, with clear route transitions and layered entrances that reinforce structure. Avoid generic blue-purple SaaS styling, neon dark mode, heavy glass effects, and noisy decorative gimmicks.

### Design Principles
1. Make operational work feel precise and calm.
2. Create hierarchy through typography, rhythm, and surfaces before using color.
3. Use motion to explain state changes and navigation, never to show off.
4. Preserve readability in dense business views with stable spacing and numeric clarity.
5. Keep accessibility intact with strong contrast, visible focus, and reduced-motion support.
